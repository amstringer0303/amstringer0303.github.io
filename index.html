Research

I study landfast sea ice dynamics and coastal climate risk in Arctic communities. My work combines satellite observations, machine learning, and community knowledge to understand how environmental change affects travel routes and subsistence access.

Current work includes developing a U-Net model to detect landfast ice edges from Sentinel-2 imagery and analyzing freeze-up dynamics in Arctic coastal systems.

Kivalina — Master's Research

Master's research examining sea ice thickness and subsistence access using ICESat-2 altimetry and community interviews in Kivalina, Alaska.

Qikiqtarjuaq Field School

Field training in Arctic glaciology and environmental observation in Qikiqtarjuaq, Nunavut.

Utqiaġvik Research Scoping — Aug 2025

Research planning and environmental observation along Alaska's Arctic coast.
