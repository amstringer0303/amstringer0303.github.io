Research

I study landfast sea ice dynamics and coastal climate risk in Arctic communities. My work combines satellite observations, machine learning, and community knowledge to understand how environmental change affects travel routes and subsistence access.

Current work includes developing a U-Net model to detect landfast ice edges from Sentinel-2 imagery and analyzing influences on ice formation in Arctic coastal systems. I am also working on trail access and mobility modeling for Alaskan communities through a grant out of the University of Leeds.

I grew up in Utqiaġvik, Alaska and am deeply committed to community-engaged research, using current and emerging technologies to support those living in Arctic communities.
