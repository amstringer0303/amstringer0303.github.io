Ana Stringer

I study Arctic coastal systems and how sea ice change affects community mobility and climate risk. My research combines satellite remote sensing, machine learning, and field observations.

Research

My doctoral work focuses on automated detection of landfast ice edges using Sentinel-2 imagery and U-Net models, and examining how freeze-up dynamics influence mobility across Arctic coastal communities.

Fieldwork

Research locations include Kivalina, Utqiaġvik, and Qikiqtarjuaq. Field campaigns combine environmental observation, satellite validation, and community-informed research approaches.
