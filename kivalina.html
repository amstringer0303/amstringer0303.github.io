Kivalina, Alaska

Fieldwork in Kivalina during 2023–2024 formed part of my M.S. thesis research at the University of Washington.

This project examined how changing sea ice thickness and freeze-up timing influence subsistence access and travel routes for coastal communities.

The research combined ICESat-2 satellite altimetry, remote sensing analysis, and community interviews to understand how environmental change affects mobility and risk across Arctic sea-ice trails.
