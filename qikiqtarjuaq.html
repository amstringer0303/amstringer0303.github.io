Qikiqtarjuaq, Nunavut

Advanced Field School on Arctic Sea Ice · April 2025

In April 2025, I participated in the Advanced Field School on Arctic Sea Ice in Qikiqtarjuaq, Nunavut. The field school focused on tracking sea ice change across scales and brought together students and researchers working across remote sensing, marine systems, Arctic environmental science, and related fields.

Hosted at the Qikiqtarjuaq Research Station, the program combined lectures, hands-on field activities, and interdisciplinary discussion centered on Arctic sea ice. The field school examined sea ice from small-scale structure to broader landscape patterns, with attention to how changing ice conditions shape ecosystems, northern communities, and climate processes.

The experience provided training in field-based observation of sea ice and exposure to cross-disciplinary Arctic research approaches. It also emphasized engagement with Inuit knowledge and community perspectives on sea ice, environmental change, and the challenges northern communities face under climate variability and warming conditions.
